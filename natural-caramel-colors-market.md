<p><p>The natural caramel colors market is categorized into four classes based on their production methods and application. Class I is produced using only heat, ideal for light applications. Class II is heat-treated in the presence of acids or salts, providing stronger color for soft drinks. Class III employs ammonia, resulting in a darker shade suitable for baked goods. Class IV, the darkest, uses sulfites and is used in products like sauces and gravies, offering maximum intensity and stability.</p></p>
