<p><p>The wearable robotic exoskeleton market encompasses two primary applications: passive and active (powered) exoskeletons. Passive exoskeletons rely on mechanical structures to enhance human movement through energy redistribution, providing support and reducing fatigue in activities such as lifting or prolonged standing. In contrast, active exoskeletons utilize motors and sensors to assist or augment movements, offering greater mobility and strength for rehabilitation, industrial tasks, and military applications. Both types aim to improve performance, reduce injury risk, and enhance quality of life.</p></p>
