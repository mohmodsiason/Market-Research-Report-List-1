Surgical suturing and stapling devices involve traditional methods for wound closure, utilizing sutures and staples to join tissues. In contrast, electrosurgical sealing devices employ electrical currents to coagulate tissue and create seals, enhancing precision and minimizing bleeding. Both types aim to improve surgical outcomes, reduce recovery times, and promote healing, catering to a range of surgical specialties and patient needs.</p></p>
