<p><ul><li>Low-level (LLW)</li><li>Intermediate-level (ILW)</li><li>High-level (HLW)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The radioactive medical waste management market is categorized into three types based on the level of radioactivity. Low-level waste (LLW) consists of materials like contaminated clothing and lab equipment, requiring minimal shielding during handling. Intermediate-level waste (ILW) includes items like spent nuclear medicine sources, necessitating more robust containment and management solutions. High-level waste (HLW), primarily generated from radioactive decay products and spent fuel, poses significant radiological hazards, demanding advanced storage, transportation, and disposal strategies to ensure safety and environmental protection.</p></p>
