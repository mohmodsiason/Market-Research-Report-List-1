<p><p>The alumina tubular ceramic membrane market is categorized into microfiltration and ultrafiltration types. Microfiltration involves larger pore sizes, effectively removing suspended solids, bacteria, and larger microorganisms, making it suitable for applications like water treatment and food processing. Conversely, ultrafiltration features smaller pore sizes, allowing for the separation of macromolecules, proteins, and smaller contaminants while retaining essential substances. This technology is widely utilized in pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, and wastewater treatment, emphasizing the versatility and efficiency of alumina ceramic membranes in various industries.</p></p>
