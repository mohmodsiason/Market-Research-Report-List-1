<p><p>The UV phototherapy light market consists of two primary types: broadband UV and narrowband UV. Broadband UV therapy utilizes a wide spectrum of ultraviolet light, typically ranging from 180 to 400 nm, making it suitable for various skin conditions but may cause more side effects. In contrast, narrowband UV light focuses on a specific wavelength, typically around 311 nm, providing targeted treatment for skin disorders like psoriasis with fewer side effects, enhancing the therapeutic efficacy and patient safety.</p></p>
